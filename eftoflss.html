Predicting EFTofLSS Counter-terms from Halo Properties

Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee • IISER Pune (Aug 2025 - Present)

Project Overview & Motivation

The Effective Field Theory (EFT) approach for studying LSS has been successful in modeling the large-scale universe as an effective fluid while including the effects from non-linear scales. It has been proposed to formulate a well-defined and controlled alternative to conventional cosmological perturbation theory.

The EFT of LSS approach begins by coarse-graining the standard cosmological fluid equations, which introduces an effective stress tensor sourced by short-scale dynamics. The back-reaction on the long-wavelength fields due to non-linear motions is precisely what generates the effective stress tensor.

This effective stress tensor can be written both as an expansion in terms of long-wavelength fields, as well as in terms of short-wavelength fields. Terms in the long-wavelength expansion are written purely based on the symmetries of the theory, with undetermined coefficients known as the counter-terms. In general, these counter-terms are measured through cosmological N-body simulations.

When taking expectation values over these short-wavelength modes in the presence of long-wavelength perturbations, the symmetries of the system constrain the corresponding long-wavelength expansion. The effective stress tensor takes the form of an imperfect fluid:

\begin{align} \left\langle \left[ \tau^{ij} \right]_{\Lambda} \right\rangle_{\delta_l} &= p_b \, \delta^{ij} + \rho_b \Bigg[ c_s^2 \, \delta_l \, \delta^{ij} - \frac{c_{bv}^2}{Ha} \, \delta^{ij} \, \partial_k v_l^k \\ &\quad - \frac{3}{4} \frac{c_{sv}^2}{Ha} \left( \partial^j v_l^i + \partial^i v_l^j - \frac{2}{3} \delta^{ij} \, \partial_k v_l^k \right) \Bigg] + \Delta \tau^{ij} + \cdots \end{align}

Here, \( p_b \) represents background pressure, \( c_s^2 \) denotes the speed of sound of the fluctuations, while \( c_{bv} \) and \( c_{sv} \) correspond to the bulk and shear viscosity parameters respectively.

The goal of this research project is to map out the explicit physical origins of these counter-terms. Instead of treating counter-terms purely as nuisance parameters to be fitted to observational data, we look directly inside large-scale N-body simulations to calculate them deterministically from the aggregate properties of individual dark matter halos.

Methodology & Theoretical Framework

To trace the precise footprint of short-scale contributions, we decouple the kinematics inside our GADGET-4 simulation setups. The short-wavelength component of the effective stress tensor is quantified directly using localized sub-resolution properties:

\[ \tau_{ij} = \rho_m v_i^s v_j^s + \frac{2 \partial_i\phi^s \partial_j\phi^s - \delta_{ij} \partial_k\phi^s \partial_k\phi^s}{8 \pi G} \]

By processing halo mass profiles, localized velocity configurations, and gravitational potentials derived from individual clusters, we track how aggregate non-linear properties scale up into these fundamental tensor frameworks.
