Predicting EFTofLSS Counter-terms from Halo Properties

Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee • IISER Pune (Aug 2025 - Present)

Project Overview & Motivation

The Effective Field Theory of Large-Scale Structure (EFTofLSS) provides a rigorous mathematical framework to model the mildly non-linear regime of cosmic structure formation. However, a major bottleneck in standard EFT applications is the emergence of free, macroscopic parameter sets known as **counter-terms**. These counter-terms absorb small-scale, highly non-linear dynamics that analytical perturbation theory cannot resolve natively.

The goal of this research project is to map out the explicit physical origins of these parameters. Instead of treating counter-terms purely as nuisance parameters to be fitted to observational data, we look directly inside large-scale N-body simulations ($GADGET-4$) to calculate them deterministically from the aggregate properties of individual dark matter halos.

Methodology & Theoretical Framework

Our computational pipeline isolates individual dark matter halos within large box volumes to track short-scale configurations. We map long-wavelength fields alongside the underlying effective stress tensor calculated via:

τij(x) = ρ(x) vi(x) vj(x) + pij(x)

By correlating spatial halo properties—such as local mass density profiles, halo spin parameter metrics, and internal velocity dispersions—we build an assignment framework designed to output physically motivated, accurate estimations of these cosmological counter-terms across varying resolution boundaries.

Current Progress & Preliminary Results

  • Pipeline Implementation: Constructed data parsing tools to filter large snapshot files generated from N-body boxes.
  • Feature Extraction: Calculated localized stress tensors for thousands of halo instances across specific mass bins.
  • Next Horizons: Currently implementing testing sequences to evaluate if incorporating velocity dispersion fields bounds prediction errors more closely compared to mass-only assignments.
