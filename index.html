Research Experience & Interests

Predicting EFTofLSS Counter-terms from Halo Properties
Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee, IISER Pune (Aug 2025 - Present)

Developing a framework to investigate the physical origins of counter-terms in the Effective Field Theory of Large Scale Structure. By computing the effective stress tensor for individual dark matter halos within large-scale N-body simulations, we aim to bridge the gap between non-linear short-scale dynamics and long-wavelength cosmological fields.

Modeling the HI Distribution in the Post-Reionization Universe
Summer Project with Prof. Jasjeet Singh Bagla, IISER Mohali (May 2025 - July 2025)

Developed a statistical framework to predict the spatial distribution of neutral hydrogen using Illustris TNG simulations. Implemented dual-stage Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and linear regression to create a physically-motivated estimator using halo mass, spin, and velocity dispersion.

Baryonic Effects on the Matter Distribution in Cosmology
Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee, IISER Pune (Aug 2024 - April 2025)

Investigated the impact of baryonic feedback on large-scale matter distribution by comparing theoretical models with cosmological simulations utilizing the Spherical Collapse model.
