Research Experience & Interests

Predicting EFTofLSS Counter-terms from Halo Properties
Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee, IISER Pune (Aug 2025 - Present)

Developing a framework to investigate the physical origins of counter-terms in the Effective Field Theory of Large Scale Structure. By computing the effective stress tensor for individual dark matter halos within large-scale N-body simulations, we aim to bridge the gap between non-linear short-scale dynamics and long-wavelength cosmological fields. We focus on correlating halo properties in N-body simulations to the stress tensor, for a physically-motivated estimation of counter-terms.

Modeling the HI Distribution in the Post-Reionization Universe
Summer Project with Prof. Jasjeet Singh Bagla, IISER Mohali (May 2025 - July 2025)

Developed a statistical framework to predict the spatial distribution of neutral hydrogen using Illustris TNG simulations. Implemented dual-stage Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and linear regression to create a physically-motivated estimator using halo mass, spin, and velocity dispersion. The model improved accuracy over traditional mass-assignment methods across multiple redshifts and effectively incorporated both central and satellite populations.

Baryonic Effects on the Matter Distribution in Cosmology
Semester Project with Dr. Arka Banerjee, IISER Pune (Aug 2024 - April 2025)

Investigated the impact of baryonic feedback on large-scale matter distribution by comparing theoretical models with cosmological simulations. We utilised the Spherical Collapse model to characterize dark matter PDFs under non-linear gravitational evolution. The aim was to find quantities related to the matter distribution PDFs that remain conserved through gravitational evolution, providing a technique to decouple baryonic signatures from gravitational dynamics.
